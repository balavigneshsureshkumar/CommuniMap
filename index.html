About CommuniMap

GALLANT Project Overview

GALLANT (Glasgow as a Living Lab Accelerating Novel Transformation) is a University project aiming to design novel ways to adapt to climate change. CommuniMap is a digital tool that acts as a data repository for community science data, allowing users to share travel patterns, map routes, flag barriers, and highlight safe, joyful routes.

Challenge

Our Problem

Encouraging people to reduce CO₂ emissions by choosing active travel (walking, cycling, etc) over motorised transport requires more than just tracking their routes; it requires motivation and engagement.

Our Goal

To design a gamified companion solution that leverages data from GALLANT’s CommuniMap Mobile Application to promote active travel and raise awareness of carbon savings. By externally calculating CO₂ offsets from journey data and incorporating features like leaderboards, group challenges, and social incentives, we aim to engage users through behavior-driven design. This system will emphasize both measurable environmental impact and trustworthiness in tracking, aligning with stakeholder goals for sustainability, user motivation, and scalable software integration.

Our Requirements

Behavioral Change

How do we encourage users to choose walking and cycling over motor vehicles?

Gamification

How can we design a trustworthy gamified system with leaderboards and group dynamics?

CO₂ Tracking

How do we accurately calculate and display CO₂ savings from active travel?

What We Learnt

This is what we have learnt from our 3 weeks of OIP, from talking to stakeholders to building prototypes.

Talking to Stakeholders

  • Questions are more important than answers: When talking to Dr Sarah from GALLANT, we found that asking the right questions will reveal the true user needs and constraints on a deeper level. Using "How might we...", "Why...", or "What if" questions will open discussions and allows people to think differently. For example, instead of "Would you use a CO2 tracker?", we used "How might we help users see the impact of their active travel?". We learnt that a well-crafted question helps to unlock genuine insights for useful solutions. The Double Diamond method that we learnt was proved to be useful for questioning about our project.
  • Engage with Stakeholders/Users Effectively: During our second partner meeting, we pitched our ideas and asked Dr Sarah for her feedback and insights. She challenged our assumptions, added new considerations offered new directions. From this, we learnt that Stakeholders is not just about "approval or rejection" of ideas. They are partners throughout the whole design process and should not be seen as "clients". They offer valuable insights, to a point where we wished we had engaged with them more frequently. Stakeholders co-shape our ideas, not just validate them.
  • Clear and Relatable Communication: When presenting our ideas/prototypes, we used real-world examples/analogies/terms to explain our features. For example, we explained that our Virtual Pet system is similar to a Tamagotchi, Navigation feature is similar to Google Maps. We learnt that this helps to connect with the stakeholder/user's experience and mental model which enables us to design with the average user's perspective. This communication style allowed the stakeholder/user to understand and respond more meaningfully. In return, we get more meaningful insights/feedback.

    • Building Prototypes

  • Every team member's perspective needs to be heard: We found that each member brings different experiences, biases, strengths and intuition. The different viewpoints helps to uncover blind spots in our design. We learnt that while getting the users' perspectives is important, getting the team's perspectives is equally important in making a viable solution. We want our prototype to try to satisfy all perspectives. While we acknowledge that it is impossible to satisfy all of them, we want to try to cater to as much as we can and that starts with getting the different perspectives.
  • Mobile-First Design: CommuniMap has both a website companion and a mobile application. Our problem focuses on walking/cycling which forces the focus to be on the mobile application design as it would be impossible to walk/cycle with a laptop. What we learnt from this is that we would need to take note of the mobile phone's resources such as screen constraints. Are the buttons too big or too small? Are the words legible? This shifted the team's mentality to take into account the User Interface and User Experience (UI/UX), not just the functionality of the gamification.
  • Sketch-based/Paper-based Prototyping is Effective: The main prototyping method we used in our project is sketch-based prototyping where we created wireframes of the user interfaces and flow. We found this method to be quick and easy to create and make changes as all it takes is just a pencil and paper. We learnt that this allows us to have a quick visual representation of the mobile application interface such as the buttons, bars, etc. Through this, we are able to get fast feedback and have multiple quick interations for prototyping. We also found it easy to mix-and-match elements to test concepts early. Elements were tested based on visual and functional feasibility. The quickness of this method allowed us to know if are on the right track at each iteration.

    • Proud Essay

    Read our essay about what we are proud of, or not proud of, about our work

    Project Background

    Our project mainly revolves around GALLANT's CommuniMap application and encouraging users to take up active travel instead of motorised vehicles. The purpose is to reduce CO₂ emissions within the city of Glasgow and raising awareness of the CO₂ emissions saved. This project has given us the experience of many emotions and types of challenges. The multi-cultural and cross-national nature of the project has broadened our view.

    Team Dynamics and Adaptation

    Despite having prior experience working together, we had to adapt to a new environment. The team had to adjust to different working styles and communication methods, which was initially challenging. However, effective collaboration and understanding helped us overcome these hurdles.

    Collaboration with Dr. Sarah (Key Stakeholder)

    Working with Dr. Sarah, a key stakeholder in the GALLANT project, has been an incredibly positive experience. She has been exceptionally supportive and generous with her time, helping us gain a deeper understanding of user requirements through face-to-face meetings and prompt, thoughtful responses to our unscheduled emails. Her insights into the habits and challenges of CommuniMap users have been invaluable to the development of our project.

    Adapting to Glasgow's Lifestyle and Culture

    This project was a novel experience for us as there was no similar app back in Singapore. We had to adapt our thinking to fit the Glasgow lifestyle, understanding local transportation habits and roadblocks by spending time in the city.

    What We Are Proud Of

    • The Final Prototype
      We are proud of the gamified version of the CommuniMap application that we created. The prototype allows users to engage with virtual pets, forming a fun and motivating relationship that encourages user to use the app more consistently.

    • Team Collaboration
      The collaborative effort from every team member was key to our success. Each person brought their unique skills and perspectives, helping us create a cohesive and engaging prototype. We iterated on our designs multiple times, ensuring that the final product was both user-friendly and visually appealing.

    • Effort and Dedication
      We are proud of the effort and dedication we invested in this project. We worked tirelessly to ensure that our prototype met the project's objectives and aligned with the requirements.

    What We Are Not Proud Of

    • Time and Resource Constraints
      We feel it could have been enhanced with more time and resources. Due to time limitations, we had to compromise on certain features and functionalities, which we believe could have been more polished with additional work.

    • Focus Narrowing
      Dr. Sarah provided valuable feedback, noting that our initial ideas were all very good. Due to the time constraints, we had to focus on just one idea for the final prototype, which mean some of the original vision couldn't be fully realized.

    • Room for Improvement
      We recognize that there's always room for growth. We acknowledge that the design process is an ongoing journey, and with more time and resources, we could refine and improve the prototype even further.

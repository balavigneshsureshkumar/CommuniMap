About CommuniMap

GALLANT Project Overview

GALLANT (Glasgow as a Living Lab Accelerating Novel Transformation) is a University project aiming to design novel ways to adapt to climate change. CommuniMap is a digital tool that acts as a data repository for community science data, allowing users to share travel patterns, map routes, flag barriers, and highlight safe, joyful routes.

The Challenge

Promoting Active Travel & CO‚ÇÇ Awareness

There is interest in finding ways to promote active travel and spread awareness of the amount of carbon dioxide (CO‚ÇÇ) saved from entering the environment through each active journey taken.

Behavioral Change

Gamification

Our Solution: Virtual Pet Gamification

How We Gamified CommuniMap

We implemented a virtual pet system that users can customize using points they gather from walking or cycling on their daily routes. The points represent their carbon offset from choosing to walk or cycle over taking private transportation.

22 July 2025

Project Requirement Gathering

  • The project kicked off with a team meeting to discuss about our given project: Active Travel CO2 with GALLANT.
  • The team conducted a brainstorming session to outline the key objectives and deliverables for the project.
  • An initial research was done to know the current context of GALLANT and Glasgow.

    • Stakeholder Meeting

  • A meeting was scheduled with Dr Sarah from GALLANT, who will be our main partner and stakeholder in this project.
  • A set of questions was drafted before the meeting to ensure that we were able to get valuable insights from Dr Sarah. The questions were drafted using what we had learnt during the first lecture that includes "How might we..." questions and the Double Diamond method.
  • During the meeting, we discussed the project requirements, expectations, and potential challenges.
  • After the stakeholder meeting, we had a small team meeting to debrief and discuss the key takeaways from the meeting with Dr Sarah to ensure that the whole team is on the same page.

    • 23 July 2025

    Role Allocation

  • First thing the team did today was to divide the tasks and allocate roles for each member to start working on the project.
  • 2 main groups of tasks were formed within the team: Prototyping and Video Pitch.
  • Prototyping team was responsible for creating ideas and quick sketches of potential solutions.
  • Video Pitch team was responsible for creating the Weekly Pitch Videos that reflect on what we have learnt from talking to stakeholders and building prototypes.
  • Vignesh, Kee Shen, Bruce and Jovian were assigned to the Prototyping team while Felix and Lin Feng were assigned to the Video Pitch team.
  • While the members have been divided, it was also agreed that the two teams would help one another and be flexible on the roles as needed.

    • Team Contract

  • To further ensure our commitment to the team project, a team contract was created and was signed by all members.
  • The team contract includes our team values, communication methods, and expectations for each member.
  • This team contract was subsequently submitted on Moodle.

    • 24 July 2025

    Research on Project Requirements

  • When brainstorming ideas for the project, the team realised that further research is needed based on what was learnt from the meeting with Dr Sarah
  • Further research include the current state of active travel in Glasgow, the incentives or motivations, GALLANT's CommuniMap application and more
  • We dedicated time to fully understand the user needs and pain points before moving on to the ideation phase.

    • 25 July 2025

    Prototype Ideation

  • To end off the first week of the OIP, the team held a brainstorming session to generate ideas for the prototypes.
  • We used the Double Diamond method to structure our ideation process, focusing on defining the problem and exploring potential solutions.
  • Solutions include how to incentivise and motivate users to do active travel and how to make them more engaged with the CommuniMap applications
  • Potential ideas include gamification of the application, calculating the CO2 emissions saved for their route, rewarding users with shopping vouchers for reaching a certain amount of CO2 saved, and more.
  • While brainstorming, quick sketches were made to visualize the ideas and concepts being discussed.

    • Pitch Video 1

  • A Powerpoint document was created to facilitate the presentation of our reflection for this week's pitch video.
  • Throughout the ideation process, the team also wrote what we learnt from this process onto the document.
  • What we learnt from talking to Dr Sarah from GALLANT was also written down onto the Powerpoint document.
  • Subsequently, the team reviewed the document and recorded it as a voiceover video for submission on Moodle.

    • 28 July 2025

    Creating sketches from Ideas

  • We drew up many different screens based on the discussions and ideations from the previous week.
  • These sketches were created to visualize the ideas and concepts we had discussed, including virtual pet system, routing capabilities, and achievement systems.
  • We focused on how the virtual pet would enhance the active travelling aspect and build an attachment with users, how CO‚ÇÇ savings would be calculated, and how users could earn points through active travel.
  • These sketches served as the foundation for our prototype development in the coming weeks.

    • 29 July 2025

    Sketches to Figma

  • We took the sketches created yesterday and started to digitize them using Figma.
  • Using the main screens of the CommuniMap application, we adapted our ideas into the screens, focusing on the user interface and experience, ensuring that the design was inituitive and engaging
  • We also discussed the flow of our prototype, how the virtual pet, in app currency (CO‚ÇÇ coins), quests and leaderboards, would be integrated into the application.
  • Prototyping the flow allows us to visualize how the different systems and features work together and evolve based on the user's active travel.

    • 30 July 2025

    Start of Poster and Website

  • We started working on the project poster and website to showcase our progress and ideas.
  • The poster was designed to highlight our project goals, the problem we are addressing, and the solutions we are proposing.
  • The website was created to provide a platform for sharing our project development journey and reflections.
  • We experimented with different designs, aesthetics, and layouts to see which combinations best fit with the nature of our prototype

    • 31 July 2025

    Stakeholder Meeting 2

  • We had a second meeting with Dr Sarah to present our initial ideas and sketches.
  • During the meeting, we presented our sketches and presented on the different ideas and prototypes we created.
  • We also discussed the potential challenges and limitations of our proposed solutions, including how to ensure that our prototype can be progressed in different phases to ease users with the ideas we introduced
  • After the meeting, we had a team debrief to discuss the feedback and how we could incorporate it into our prototype development.

    • Feedback from Stakeholders

  • We received positive feedback on our initial ideas and sketches, especially with the virtual pet and route finder ideas
  • Dr Sarah appreciated our focus on user experience and the potential of the virtual pet system to help users build an attachment to the app.
  • Additionally, she said that the current userbase would highly favour towards the route finder idea
  • Dr Sarah also highlighted the relationship building aspect of the app, emphasizing the importance of kepping momentum in retention of users
  • We also talked about the importance of making the CO‚ÇÇ savings calculation transparent and easy to understand for users.
  • Overall, the feedback was constructive and helped us refine our ideas further.

    • 1 August 2025

    Improving Prototypes, Poster and Website

  • We took the feedback from Dr Sarah and started to improve our prototypes, poster, and website.
  • We focused on improving the virtual pet aspect, as it was the novel idea which could be introduced early to help build relationship with users
  • We also worked on refining and editing the colour palette of the screens, making it more user-friendly and fit the colours of the GALLANT brand.
  • Additionally, we made improvements to the poster and website, adding more details from our journey so far and ensuring that they effectively communicated our project goals and progress.

    • Pitch Video 2

  • A Powerpoint document was created to facilitate the presentation of our reflection for this week's pitch video.
  • Throughout the process, the team wrote what we learnt from this process onto the document.
  • What we learnt from talking to Dr Sarah from GALLANT was also written down onto the Powerpoint document.
  • Subsequently, the team reviewed the document and recorded it as a voiceover video for submission on Moodle.

    • 4 August 2025

    Usability Testing

  • We conducted usability testing with a small group of users to gather feedback on our prototype, giving them a list of tasks to execute.
  • During the testing, we observed how users interacted with the prototype and gathered their feedback on the user interface, features, and overall experience.
  • Based on the feedback, many expressed positively to the virtual pet idea, praising its aesthetics and appeal.

    • 5 August 2025

    Finalising Prototypes and Poster

  • The team spent the day finalizing the design and layout of the poster and prototype
  • Overall improvements were on the aesthetics and layout
  • We also made final adjustments to the descriptions in the poster, ensuring that it effectively communicated our prototype's goals.

    • Feedback from Teaching Assistants

  • The feedback we received was that our thought process could be made clearer and more obvious to help readers understand the intentions behind our designs
  • The feedback was constructive and helped us refine our ideas further.
  • We discussed the feedback as a team and made adjustments to our prototype and poster based on the suggestions.
  • This iterative process of feedback and refinement is crucial and helped us to ensure that our final prototype meets the project requirements, objectives, user needs.

    • 6 August 2025

    Finalising Website

  • We showcase our design process on the website, by including sketches, prototypes, and poster designs.
  • We modify the layout and change the color tone to ensure a more visually appealing for user experience across all devices.
  • We also adjust the website to be responsive and mobile-friendly.
